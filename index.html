About Me

Front-End Developer, Graphic Designer & Social Impact Technologist

I am a purpose-driven Front-End Developer, Graphic Designer, and Creative Technologist with over 7 years of experience blending technical precision with human-centered design to create meaningful digital solutions. My work lies at the intersection of technology, community empowerment, and visual storytelling.

As the Founder of Nguvu TEACH EdTech and Co-Founder of KI4BLI, I have championed digital transformation among refugees and marginalised African youth by delivering high-quality training in web development, graphic design, entrepreneurship, and digital literacy. Through these initiatives, I aim to break cycles of exclusion and build bridges toward opportunity.

Learn More About My Mission and

My mission is to create impactful visual and digital solutions that combine creativity with technical precision to inspire, educate, and transform underserved communities. I believe the lack of access to digital skills and technology among refugee and marginalised populations is one of the most pressing challenges of our time — and addressing it is not just my profession, it's my mandate.

With formal education in Computer Science, Community Development, Biblical Studies, and Nutrition, and certifications in Front-End Development, Data Protection, Leadership, Counseling, and Entrepreneurship, I bring both vision and versatility to every project. I’m also trained in Organisational Board Governance, contributing to institutional development and strategic leadership.

My vision is of a world where every refugee and marginalised individual has access to quality education, digital tools, and economic empowerment. I’m passionate about building that future—one design, one line of code, one student at a time.
